# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want participation in this project to be a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Showing empathy and kindness toward other people
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully
- Respecting different viewpoints, experiences, and skill levels
- Taking responsibility for mistakes, apologizing, and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the project and community

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or demeaning conduct
- Trolling, insults, personal or political attacks, or inflammatory comments
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing other people's private information without permission
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, wiki edits, discussions, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki content, release notes, and other community channels. It also applies when someone is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

To report unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers at the admin contact configured for the deployment or open a private security-style report through the channel described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) if public reporting would expose sensitive details. Reports will be reviewed and handled as promptly and fairly as possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may use the following guidelines when determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Community impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning describing the issue and the expected change in behavior.

### 2. Warning

**Community impact:** A single incident or series of actions that disrupts the community.

**Consequence:** A warning with clear expectations for future behavior. Continued conduct may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community impact:** A serious violation, including sustained inappropriate behavior or harassment.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from interacting with the project or community for a defined period.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community impact:** A pattern of violations or a severe incident such as harassment, discrimination, or threats.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from participating in the project community.
